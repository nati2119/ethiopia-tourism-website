The Land of Origins

Ethiopia is one of the oldest independent countries in Africa and one of the oldest in the world. It is the land of the Queen of Sheba, the Ark of the Covenant, and some of the earliest hominid fossils ever discovered.

With a history dating back over 3,000 years, Ethiopia is the only African country that was never colonized, maintaining its independence throughout the Scramble for Africa.

The country is home to diverse ethnic groups, each with its own language, culture, and traditions. Ethiopia is also the birthplace of coffee, and the Ethiopian coffee ceremony is an integral part of social and cultural life.

9 UNESCO Sites

Ethiopia boasts 9 UNESCO World Heritage sites, more than any other country in Africa.

Diverse Landscape

From the highest peaks to the lowest depressions, Ethiopia's terrain is incredibly diverse.

13 Month Calendar

Ethiopia follows its own calendar with 13 months, 12 of which have 30 days and one with 5 or 6.

Ethiopian History Timeline

c. 4th century BC

Kingdom of D'mt

One of the earliest known kingdoms in Ethiopia, known for its advanced irrigation systems and monumental architecture.

c. 100 AD - 940 AD

Aksumite Empire

The Aksumite Empire was a major trading nation and one of the four great powers of its time, adopting Christianity in the 4th century.

12th century

Rock-Hewn Churches of Lalibela

King Lalibela commissions 11 monolithic churches carved from solid rock, creating a "New Jerusalem" for Christians.

1936-1941

Italian Occupation

Brief Italian occupation during WWII, but Ethiopia maintains its status as never being colonized.
