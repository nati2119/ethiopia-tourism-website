Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about traveling to Ethiopia.

Yes, most visitors require a visa to enter Ethiopia. Tourist visas can be obtained online through the official e-visa portal or at Ethiopian embassies abroad. Some nationalities can also get a visa on arrival at Bole International Airport in Addis Ababa.

The best time to visit Ethiopia is during the dry season from October to March. This period offers pleasant weather and clear skies, ideal for trekking and sightseeing. The rainy season is from June to September, which can make some roads impassable but offers lush green landscapes.

Ethiopia is generally safe for tourists, especially in major tourist areas. However, like any destination, it's important to take standard precautions. We recommend traveling with reputable tour operators, avoiding political demonstrations, and following local advice. Our team provides 24/7 support for all our clients.

Recommended vaccinations include Yellow Fever (required if coming from a Yellow Fever endemic country), Typhoid, Hepatitis A and B, Tetanus, and Meningitis. Malaria prophylaxis is also recommended for travel to lowland areas. Please consult with a travel clinic or your doctor at least 4-6 weeks before your trip.
